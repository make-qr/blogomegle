Blind Date at Pike Place — Late Bloom Stories Part VIII

Tyler & Faith in Seattle — Friends made me go. A Late Bloom love story with a happy ending.

    “Friends made me go”

    This is Part VIII of Late Bloom Stories — 300 episodes of romance with happy endings, loneliness, random chat, and courage to reach out again. After Part VII.

    Seattle

    This is the story of Tyler and Faith.

    When life got in the way

    Tyler worked as a rideshare driver. Faith was a librarian.

    They met near Pike Place in Seattle — not like a movie. Two tired adults whose worlds shrank until they sat close enough to talk.

    Evenings became walks. Morning texts. Are you home yet? Love without fireworks — the kind that grows because both people had already learned what losing feels like.

    Then life broke in. The person across the table was her ex.

    Not because love ended. Because life is heavier than feeling sometimes — family, money, pride, timing, fear.

    Tyler let go first — thinking he was protecting Faith. He said the hardest sentence he could find. She cried, or went quiet. They parted without a ritual goodbye. Only space.

    On the loneliest nights, Tyler opened his browser — a random text chat, no app required. Not to find someone new. Not to replace Faith. Just to hear a stranger say hey — proof that another human was awake in Seattle.

    Lonely nights, random chat

    Faith did the same. She tried voice chat after midnight — they had already talked anonymously about the wedding they dreaded this weekend. They never met on that screen. Never traded real names. A few sentences, then close the tab — loneliness still there, but a little lighter.

    They did not know the person they would meet offline was sometimes the one they needed all along. Random chat does not create love. It keeps the heart from freezing completely — so when love returns, there is still room for it.

    Time passed. Tyler rebuilt a modest life. Faith did too. They did not delete numbers. They did not call. They did not forget.

    One day at Pike Place, chance — or something like it — put them in the same frame again.

    The same unusual phrase appeared in both chat logs.

    Someone said: Friends made me go — the line only former lovers use when pretending to be strangers.

    The ending you hoped for

    They sat down. Told the truth. Random chat had softened lonely nights — but only presence could finish the story.

    A second date — nobody forced them this time. A year or two later they chose each other again — not from loneliness. From love.
