Omegle shut down in November 2023. Search traffic didn't — millions still type **"omegle alternative"** every month. The names that fill results aren't equal. Here's a practical comparison for 2026.

## What people miss about Omegle

- Instant match, no account
- Text and video in one place
- Simple "Stranger" / "You" layout

Any replacement should be judged on: **speed to first chat**, **mobile browser support**, **moderation tools**, and **privacy clarity**.

## Chatroulette

**Pros:** Original random video concept, brand recognition, long history  
**Cons:** Reputation mixed; moderation quality varies by era and region  
**Best for:** Users who want classic video roulette and accept the chaos

**Check before use:** HTTPS, report flow, whether you need an account for basic chat.

## Emerald Chat

**Pros:** Interest tags, text + video, active marketing in search results  
**Cons:** Feature gates and signup prompts on some flows; read privacy policy carefully  
**Best for:** People who want filters/interests beyond pure randomness

## OmeTV / mobile-first clones

**Pros:** Strong mobile apps, large user base in some regions  
**Cons:** Often push app installs; verify store listing and permissions  
**Best for:** Mobile-native users comfortable with app-based chat

## Browser-first options (including OmegleChat)

**Pros:** No install, works on desktop and mobile browsers, quick test drive  
**Cons:** Smaller scale than peak Omegle — match times vary by hour  
**Best for:** Privacy-conscious users who want to try before installing anything

## Comparison checklist (use on any site)

| Question | Why it matters |
|----------|----------------|
| HTTPS only? | Basic security |
| Report / block / next? | Exit bad matches fast |
| Privacy policy published? | Know who processes data |
| Forced app or crypto? | Often a scam signal |
| Works in mobile browser? | Most users are on phones |
